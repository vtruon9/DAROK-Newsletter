Dear DAROK members,

Welcome to the first edition of the DAROK Newsletter! This newsletter seeks to inform DAROK members and American voters about DAROK events, issues domestic and international that affect U.S. voters in Korea, elections in the U.S., how to register and vote from abroad, and how to donate to DAROK.

DAROK NEWS & EVENTS 📰

Town-Hall Summer Tour
 

During the summer, members of the DAROK Executive Committee and volunteers from Seoul Chapter travelled to Pyeongtaek, Busan, Daegu, and Jeonju to meet with Americans and discuss policy concerns, the importance of voting in upcoming elections, and ways to get involved with Democrats Abroad.

At the conclusion of the trip, DAROK identified leaders and activated volunteers in three of the cities visited. Plans are in place for a town hall this October in Daejeon and another in early 2026 in Jeju. With this momentum, DAROK is preparing for a robust, nationwide GOTV effort to help take back the House in the midterms next year.
Daegu Chapter Established
 

Following the town-hall summer tour in July and August, DAROK moved to establish a new chapter in Daegu, which is Korea’s fourth largest city with a population of about 2.5 million. The city is home to U.S. Army Garrison (USAG) Daegu (including Camps Walker, George, and Henry). USAG Daegu also includes nearby Camp Carroll in Waegwan and support facilities in Busan. The area is home to American military personnel and their families, civilian contractors, as well as American English teachers.

The first Daegu Chapter event was held on Saturday, September 27th at The Key Craft. Americans in the Daegu area interested in joining DAROK or participating in events can contact us at chair.darok@gmail.com.
Monthly Meet the Dems Meetings
 

On August 16, DAROK Seoul held its monthly “Meet the Dems” get-together at the Lit Lounge in Itaewon, followed by the September “Drafts for Democracy” pub night on September 19 at Linus BBQ, also in Itaewon. Both events provide a fun, relaxed atmosphere for socializing and discussing the Take Back the House 2026 campaign, all while supporting American-run businesses.

ELECTIONS INFORMATION

Recent U.S. Election Results

2025 is an “off year” in American electoral politics, but there are several important local, state, and Congressional by-elections. The elections have an important impact on which party will hold a majority in the House and state legislatures. Local elections also determine the orientation of municipal councils, school boards, courts, and other offices.

• By-elections were held for vacant (Republican) House seats in Florida and Texas, and Republican candidates won those elections in April.

• More recently, Democrat James Walkinshaw won the by-election for Virginia's 11th Congressional District on September 9th, with 75% of the vote.

• On September 23, Democrat Adelita Grijalva won the by-election for Arizona's 7th Congressional District with 69% of the vote. The seat was held by her father, Raúl Grijalva, who passed away in March.
Upcoming U.S. Elections

• Gubernatorial elections for New Jersey and Virginia will be held on November 4.

• Following Republican Mark Green’s resignation in July, a by-election to fill Tennessee's 7th Congressional District is expected to be held in December.

MAILING YOUR BALLOT

How to Submit Your Ballot from Korea

Americans living in Korea can submit ballots for both federal and local elections through the U.S. Embassy in Seoul. To vote by mail, you will need two envelopes:

Outer envelope:
U.S. Embassy Seoul
188 Sejong-daero, Jongno-gu, Seoul 03141
Attention: American Citizen Services (Ballot)

Inner envelope:
Addressed to your local state election office using the postage-paid label, with your completed ballot enclosed. Seal the inner envelope, place it inside the outer envelope, and mail the package to the Embassy.

Transit time: Normal transit from Seoul to the U.S. is 7–10 business days.
